Experiment 6 - Predictive Parsing

Predictive parsing is a top-down parsing technique used in compiler design to recognize and parse input strings according to a given grammar. It is called "predictive" because it uses a predictive parsing table to predict the next production rule to apply based on the current input symbol.

Experiment 7 - Shift Reduce Parsing

Shift-reduce parsing is a bottom-up parsing technique used in compiler design to recognize and parse input strings according to a given grammar. It is called "shift-reduce" because it involves two operations: shifting a symbol onto the parsing stack or reducing a group of symbols on the stack to a non-terminal.

Experiment 8 - Leading and Trailing

In compiler design, "leading" and "trailing" are two sets associated with the non-terminals of a grammar. They are used to determine the possible terminals that can appear as the first or last symbols of the strings derived from those non-terminals. Leading and trailing sets are especially helpful in constructing predictive parsing tables for LL(1) grammars

Experiment 9 - LR[0] Parsing

LR(0) parsing is a bottom-up parsing technique used in compiler design to analyze and parse input strings according to a given context-free grammar. It is part of a family of LR parsing methods, which are more powerful than LL parsing methods (such as LL(1) parsing) and can handle a larger class of grammars.
